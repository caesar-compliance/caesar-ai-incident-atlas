Key Ingestion Themes

  • Systemic algorithmic bias in automated employment screening systems under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).
  • Phasing out of race-based correction factors in clinical pulmonary function testing algorithms.

Legal & Commercial Takeaways

  • Joint federal enforcements indicate that hiring platforms and automated screening tools must provide accessible, alternative evaluation methods for candidates with disabilities.
  • Relying on hiring software vendor claims of compliance does not insulate employers from regulatory liability under the ADA.
  • Race-based algorithmic correction variables in healthcare are facing intense regulatory deprecation as medical guidelines align to eliminate racial corrections.

Operational Governance Lessons

  • Mandate rigorous, documented vendor questionnaires reviewing ADA compliance, alternative assessments, and accessibility support.
  • Implement clinical validation processes to verify that healthcare software uses objective biological and clinical variables rather than crude racial categories.

Required Audit & Evidence Actions

  • Obtain and log standard VPAT (Voluntary Product Accessibility Template) documents for all third-party automated hiring software.
  • Formally log validation audit reports checking that local spirometry and clinical diagnostic equipment runs software with race-free diagnostic guidelines.
