INC-0006

AI-based resume screening tool discontinued after reportedly penalising female candidates

10 October 2018 Hiring & Employment Severity: Medium Confidence: High FM-BIAS
What Happened

According to an investigative report published by Reuters in October 2018, a large technology company had developed an internal AI-based resume screening system intended to automate candidate evaluation. According to the Reuters report, which cited multiple sources with knowledge of the matter, the system was found to penalise resumes that contained the word 'women's' (for example, in references to women's clubs or organisations) and downgraded graduates of all-women colleges. The system had reportedly been trained on historical hiring data that was predominantly drawn from male candidates, and the model was found to have learned and propagated those patterns. According to the report, the company ceased using the tool after internal teams identified the problem. The company did not publicly confirm all details of the reporting. The case is widely cited as an example of training data bias propagating into consequential employment outcomes.

AI System / Context

Internal machine learning-based resume screening and candidate ranking tool developed by a large technology company. The tool was trained on historical hiring data to rank job applicants and was used internally by recruiting teams.

Harms
  • Systemic risk of gender-correlated discrimination in candidate evaluation during the period the tool was in use
  • Potential exclusion of qualified candidates whose resumes contained gender-correlated features penalised by the model
Impact

According to the Reuters report, the company ceased using the tool after internal review identified the bias patterns. No confirmed regulatory action or formal discrimination complaint has been publicly documented in connection with this specific tool. The case is widely cited as a canonical example of training data bias propagation in consequential employment contexts and has influenced guidance on AI fairness in hiring.

Evidence Required
  • EV-012 — Bias testing record: evidence of pre-deployment or post-discovery evaluation of scoring differentials across gender-correlated features
  • EV-002 — Risk assessment: pre-deployment risk assessment for discriminatory outcome risk in employment screening AI
  • EV-005 — Testing record: pre-deployment evaluation of the tool's output quality and fairness before use in recruiting
Governance Lessons
  • AI systems trained on historical hiring or employment data risk perpetuating existing demographic imbalances; training data composition should be evaluated for bias before use in consequential screening tools.
  • Pre-deployment bias testing for employment AI tools should evaluate scoring outputs for differential impact across protected characteristics, including gender-correlated features in training data.
  • Organisations should not deploy AI-based hiring or screening tools without documented pre-deployment evaluation of fairness properties and a clear process for identifying and addressing discriminatory output patterns.
  • Human review of AI-generated candidate scores should be maintained to detect bias patterns that emerge in deployed employment screening tools.
Affected Stakeholders
  • Job candidates whose resumes were evaluated by the system during its period of use
  • Organisations using AI-based employment screening tools
  • Regulators and policy bodies overseeing employment discrimination risk in automated hiring
