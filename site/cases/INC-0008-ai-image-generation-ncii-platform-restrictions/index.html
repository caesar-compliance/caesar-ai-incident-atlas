INC-0008

AI image generation tools used to produce non-consensual intimate imagery; platforms impose restrictions following public concern

12 January 2024 Media & Content Severity: High Confidence: Medium FM-PRIVFM-SAFEFM-UNAUTH
What Happened

From 2023 onwards, multiple widely reported incidents described AI image generation tools being used to create non-consensual intimate images (NCII) of real, identifiable individuals, including both public figures and private individuals. In January 2024, Microsoft announced restrictions on photorealistic image generation capabilities in its Bing Image Creator and Designer products in response to reports of these tools being used to generate such imagery. The restrictions were described in Microsoft's public safety communications. Separately, multiple other platforms including Midjourney and Stability AI had introduced or updated content policies restricting NCII generation during 2023. The pattern prompted legislative responses in multiple jurisdictions: the UK Online Safety Act includes provisions specifically addressing AI-generated NCII, and the US DEFIANCE Act (proposed in 2024) addressed the harms. The incidents represent a systemic failure to adequately evaluate foreseeable harmful use cases — specifically NCII generation — as part of pre-deployment safety testing and product design.

AI System / Context

AI image generation models, including diffusion-based systems deployed in consumer-facing products, capable of generating photorealistic images of individuals. Platforms including Microsoft Bing Image Creator imposed restrictions on certain generation capabilities following reports of misuse.

Harms
  • Severe psychological and reputational harm to individuals depicted in AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery
  • Privacy violation through non-consensual use of individuals' likenesses
  • Risk of further distribution and harm through online sharing of AI-generated NCII
Impact

Multiple platforms imposed restrictions on photorealistic image generation capabilities following reports of NCII generation misuse. Microsoft announced restrictions in January 2024. Legislative responses were initiated in the UK (Online Safety Act provisions) and the US (DEFIANCE Act, 2024). The incidents contributed to emerging regulatory frameworks specifically addressing AI-generated intimate imagery as a distinct harm category.

Governance Lessons
  • AI image generation systems must be evaluated for foreseeable harmful use cases — including non-consensual intimate imagery generation — as part of pre-deployment safety testing before public release.
  • Platform operators should not deploy general-purpose image generation tools without safety restrictions on photorealistic generation of identifiable individuals, given the well-documented risk of NCII misuse.
  • Incident response plans for AI consumer products should include procedures for detecting and responding rapidly to emerging harmful generation patterns at scale.
  • Vendor and platform governance reviews should assess NCII generation risk as a distinct and foreseeable harm category for any product capable of generating photorealistic images of people.
Affected Stakeholders
  • Individuals whose likenesses were used to generate non-consensual intimate imagery
  • Platform operators deploying AI image generation tools
  • Regulators developing legislative responses to AI-generated NCII harm
  • Vendors of AI image generation models used in consumer products
