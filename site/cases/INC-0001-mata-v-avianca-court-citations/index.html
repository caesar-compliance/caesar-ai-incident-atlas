INC-0001

Attorney submits court filings containing AI-generated fabricated case citations

22 May 2023 Legal & Compliance Severity: Medium Confidence: High FM-HALLFM-REL
What Happened

According to a publicly available court order issued by the United States District Court for the Southern District of New York in May 2023, attorneys representing a plaintiff in a personal injury action submitted court filings that cited multiple judicial decisions that the court found to be non-existent. The court found that the citations had been generated by an AI tool and that neither the attorneys nor their supervising partners had independently verified the citations before filing. The court imposed sanctions and required the attorneys to provide attestations to the courts in which the fabricated citations had been submitted. The case is publicly identified as Mata v. Avianca, Inc., No. 22-cv-1461 (S.D.N.Y.).

AI System / Context

Large language model used as a legal research tool to identify case citations for inclusion in court filings.

Harms
  • Sanctions imposed on counsel by the court
  • Court time and resources expended addressing fabricated citations
  • Institutional scrutiny of AI use in legal practice heightened
Impact

The court imposed monetary sanctions and required written attestations from the attorneys involved. The case received extensive media coverage and prompted significant professional guidance from bar associations regarding AI tool use in legal practice.

Governance Lessons
  • Practitioners using AI tools in professional or legal contexts must independently verify all AI-generated research outputs — including citations — before relying on them in official proceedings.
  • Organisations deploying AI tools for professional use should maintain documented policies requiring human review of AI outputs before submission to third parties.
  • AI tools used for legal research or citation retrieval should be evaluated for hallucination risk before deployment in practice.
Affected Stakeholders
  • Legal practitioners using AI research tools
  • Courts and judicial system
  • Clients relying on AI-assisted legal representation
